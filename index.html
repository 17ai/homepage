Stanford Computer Science
Future Data Systems Group

As rising volumes of data continue play an increasingly central role in society, today's data-intensive systems must evolve. The primary goal of the Future Data Systems (FDS) research group is to design and build next-generation data-intensive systems that anticipate, accommodate, and enable the next class of data-intensive applications.

Our design philosophy is to seek systems solutions that are principled, practical, and high-performance. We have a number of ongoing projects on topics including:

  • Geo-distributed database designs that circumvent the laws of physics by avoiding coordination
  • Automated security exploit manufacture and patching for database-backed applications
  • New engines for large-scale, end-to-end online anomaly detection and model evaluation

People: The FDS group is led by Professor Peter Bailis.
