Future Data Systems

As rising volumes of data play an increasingly central role in society, data-intensive computing systems must evolve. The mission of the Future Data Systems (FDS) group is to 1.) design and build data-intensive systems that enable the next generation of data-intensive applications and 2.) train world-class systems researchers.

Goals: We are interested in two types of systems:

  1. Systems that are at least an order of magnitude better than the competition ("10x systems").
  2. Systems that are the first of their kind ("0 to 1 systems").
10x systems are almost always powered by smart algorithms and/or clever use (or disuse) of hardware. 0 to 1 systems are enabled by creativity, audacity, and raw ambition. We build both.

Projects: We have a growing number of ongoing projects on topics including:

  • Geo-distributed database designs that circumvent the laws of physics by avoiding coordination
  • Automated security exploit manufacture and patching for database-backed applications
  • New engines for large-scale, end-to-end online anomaly detection and model evaluation

People: Professor Peter Bailis leads the FDS group. Our intrepid students include Deepak Narayanan (PhD) and Vivek Jain (MSCS).
